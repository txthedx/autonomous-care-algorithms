# Low back pain: red flag screen and STarT Back risk stratification

Two algorithms, used in sequence. Red flag screen first. STarT Back only for non-specific LBP after red flags are addressed.

## Part 1 — Red flag screen

Red flags are clinical features that raise concern for serious underlying spinal pathology: cauda equina syndrome, malignancy, infection, or fracture. Individual red flags often have high false-positive rates (Downie 2013, BMJ; Henschke 2013, Cochrane); the synthesis of which flag, with what context, drives urgency.

### Emergency flags — suggestive of cauda equina syndrome

Same-day emergency assessment and imaging (MRI). Cauda equina syndrome is a surgical emergency where delay risks permanent neurologic injury.

| Feature | Source |
|---|---|
| Saddle anesthesia (perineal or perianal numbness) | NICE NG59; Verhagen 2016 |
| New urinary retention or overflow incontinence | NICE NG59; Verhagen 2016 |
| New fecal incontinence | NICE NG59; Verhagen 2016 |
| Bilateral leg weakness or progressive bilateral neurologic deficit | NICE NG59; Verhagen 2016 |
| Severe or progressive neurologic deficit in the lower extremities | NICE NG59; Verhagen 2016 |

### High-concern flags — suggestive of malignancy or infection

Urgent workup within days. Imaging and laboratory studies as appropriate to the clinical picture.

| Feature | Suggests | Source |
|---|---|---|
| History of cancer with new back pain | Malignancy | Henschke 2013; Downie 2013 |
| Unexplained weight loss | Malignancy | Downie 2013; Verhagen 2016 |
| Pain worse at night or unrelieved by rest | Malignancy or infection | Downie 2013 |
| Fever with back pain | Infection | NICE NG59; Verhagen 2016 |
| Intravenous drug use | Infection | NICE NG59 |
| Immunosuppression | Infection | NICE NG59 |
| Recent spinal procedure or bacteremia source | Infection | NICE NG59 |

### Moderate-concern flags — suggestive of fracture or otherwise concerning

Consider imaging and short-interval follow-up. Synthesize with other features; isolated moderate flags in an otherwise well person carry low pretest probability of serious pathology.

| Feature | Suggests | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Significant trauma (any age) | Fracture | NICE NG59; Verhagen 2016 |
| Minor trauma with osteoporosis risk (age >50, prolonged corticosteroid use, known osteoporosis) | Fracture | Downie 2013; NICE NG59 |
| Long-term corticosteroid use | Fracture | NICE NG59 |
| Age over 50 with new severe pain | Malignancy or fracture (low specificity alone) | Downie 2013 |
| Age under 18 with significant pain | Rare congenital or neoplastic causes | Verhagen 2016 |
| No improvement after 4 to 6 weeks of conservative care | Re-evaluation indicated | NICE NG59 |

### Synthesis

| Pattern | Recommended urgency |
|---|---|
| Any emergency flag | Same-day emergency assessment; MRI; do not delay for completion of other workup. |
| Any high-concern flag | Urgent workup within days; do not stratify with STarT Back. |
| Moderate flags only, no emergency or high-concern flags | Targeted workup; close follow-up; STarT Back stratification may apply once specific pathology is excluded. |
| No flags identified | Proceed to STarT Back for risk-stratified non-specific LBP management. |

## Part 2 — STarT Back Screening Tool

Developed by Hill et al. at Keele University (Hill 2008, *Arthritis Rheum*) and validated in a primary care RCT (Hill 2011, *Lancet*). Stratifies non-specific LBP into low, medium, and high risk for persistent disabling pain, matched to treatment intensity.

### Items

| Item | Statement | Score if "Agree" |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | My back pain has spread down my leg(s) at some time in the last 2 weeks. | 1 |
| 2 | I have had pain in the shoulder or neck at some time in the last 2 weeks. | 1 |
| 3 | I have only walked short distances because of my back pain. | 1 |
| 4 | In the last 2 weeks, I have dressed more slowly than usual because of back pain. | 1 |
| 5 | It is not really safe for a person with a condition like mine to be physically active. | 1 |
| 6 | Worrying thoughts have been going through my mind a lot of the time. | 1 |
| 7 | I feel that my back pain is terrible and it is never going to get any better. | 1 |
| 8 | In general I have not enjoyed all the things I used to enjoy. | 1 |
| 9 | Overall, how bothersome has your back pain been in the last 2 weeks? | 1 if "Moderately", "Very much", or "Extremely" |

- **Total score:** 0 to 9 (sum of all items).
- **Psychosocial subscale:** items 5 through 9, scoring 0 to 5.

### Risk bands

| Risk | Criteria |
|---|---|
| Low | Total score ≤ 3. |
| Medium | Total score ≥ 4 **and** psychosocial subscale ≤ 3. |
| High | Total score ≥ 4 **and** psychosocial subscale ≥ 4. |

### Management implications (Hill 2011)

| Risk band | Matched treatment used in the RCT |
|---|---|
| Low | Single session of education, reassurance, and self-management support. |
| Medium | Standardized physiotherapy targeting symptoms and function. |
| High | Combined physical and psychologically informed physiotherapy. |

The Hill 2011 trial reported reduced disability and lower cost in the stratified-care arm compared with non-stratified best practice.
