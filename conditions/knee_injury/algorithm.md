# Ottawa Knee Rule

A decision rule for selective use of radiography in adult acute knee injury, derived by Stiell et al. (1995) and prospectively validated (Stiell 1996). Approximately 100% sensitivity for clinically significant fractures in pooled analysis (Bachmann 2004).

## Pre-step — Applicability

The rule was not validated in, and should not be applied to, patients with any of the following:

| Excluding factor | Source |
|---|---|
| Age under 18 years | Stiell 1995 (adult-only derivation) |
| Isolated skin injury | Stiell 1995 |
| Gross deformity | Stiell 1995 |
| Paraplegia or multiple injuries | Stiell 1995 |
| Decreased level of consciousness | Stiell 1995 |
| Re-presentation more than 7 days after injury | Stiell 1995 |

If any factor is present, the rule does not apply; imaging and management proceed by clinical judgment.

## Criteria

**A knee radiograph series is indicated if any of the following is present:**

| Criterion | Source |
|---|---|
| Age ≥ 55 years | Stiell 1995 |
| Isolated tenderness of the patella (no other bone tenderness on examination of the knee) | Stiell 1995 |
| Tenderness at the head of the fibula | Stiell 1995 |
| Inability to flex the knee to 90° | Stiell 1995 |
| Inability to bear weight (four steps) both immediately after injury AND at presentation | Stiell 1995 |

If none of the five criteria are met, imaging is not indicated.

## Notes on the criteria

- **Isolated patellar tenderness** means the patella is the *only* bony tender point. If both the patella and the fibular head are tender, the "isolated patellar tenderness" criterion is not met, but the fibular head tenderness criterion independently triggers imaging.
- **Bearing weight** is defined as four steps, with or without a limp; inability is judged by attempt, not by refusal.
- **Range of motion** is active flexion; passive flexion is not the criterion.

## Performance

Bachmann 2004 (systematic review): pooled sensitivity for clinically significant fractures approximately 98 to 99%, specificity approximately 27%. The Pittsburgh Knee Rules (Bauer 1995) report higher specificity in some cohorts but are less widely validated.

## Pediatric note

The original derivation excluded patients under 18. The Pittsburgh Knee Rules (Bauer 1995, Moore 2005) are an alternative validated in children as young as 2 years and are not implemented in this module.
