# Wells DVT score: original (1997) and modified (2003)

Two interpretations of a single bedside scoring instrument for pretest probability of acute lower-extremity DVT, both authored by the Wells group. Current guidelines (ACCP, ESC) reference the modified two-tier form; the original three-tier form is documented here for completeness and remains in use in some settings.

## Items

| Item | Points | In 1997? | In 2003? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Active cancer (treatment within 6 months or palliative) | +1 | yes | yes |
| Paralysis, paresis, or recent plaster immobilization of a lower extremity | +1 | yes | yes |
| Recently bedridden ≥3 days, or major surgery within 12 weeks (4 weeks in 1997) | +1 | yes | yes |
| Localized tenderness along the distribution of the deep venous system | +1 | yes | yes |
| Entire leg swelling | +1 | yes | yes |
| Calf swelling >3 cm vs asymptomatic leg (measured 10 cm below tibial tuberosity) | +1 | yes | yes |
| Pitting edema confined to the symptomatic leg | +1 | yes | yes |
| Non-varicose collateral superficial veins | +1 | yes | yes |
| Previously documented DVT | +1 | **no** | yes |
| Alternative diagnosis at least as likely as DVT | **-2** | yes | yes |

Sources: Wells 1997 (*Lancet*); Wells 2003 (*NEJM*).

## Wells 1997 (original) — three-tier interpretation

Score range: -2 to 8 (the previously-documented-DVT item is not included).

| Score | Risk band | Approximate prevalence (Wells 1997) |
|---|---|---|
| ≤ 0 | Low | ~3% |
| 1 or 2 | Moderate | ~17% |
| ≥ 3 | High | ~75% |

### Recommended actions (1997 framing)

| Risk band | Action |
|---|---|
| Low | Sensitive D-dimer; if negative, DVT excluded without imaging. If positive, proceed to ultrasound. |
| Moderate | Compression ultrasound. |
| High | Compression ultrasound. Consider repeating in one week if initial study is negative and clinical suspicion persists. |

## Wells 2003 (modified) — two-tier interpretation

Score range: -2 to 9 (adds the previously-documented-DVT item, +1).

| Score | Risk band |
|---|---|
| ≤ 1 | DVT unlikely |
| ≥ 2 | DVT likely |

### Recommended actions (2003 framing, current standard)

| Risk band | Action |
|---|---|
| DVT unlikely | Sensitive (high-sensitivity) D-dimer. If negative, DVT excluded. If positive, proceed to compression ultrasound. |
| DVT likely | Proceed directly to compression ultrasound. D-dimer is not required for rule-out at this probability. |

## Notes on use

- The score performs best in symptomatic outpatients with suspected DVT, the population in which it was derived and validated.
- The D-dimer assay used in any rule-out pathway must be a high-sensitivity assay; semi-quantitative or moderate-sensitivity assays are not interchangeable.
- The 2003 modification reframed the score around a clinically actionable dichotomy (D-dimer vs ultrasound) and is the form referenced in most current guidelines.
- A negative ultrasound in a *likely*-probability patient does not rule out DVT with certainty; repeat ultrasound or further evaluation may be warranted depending on the clinical scenario.
