# CIWA-Ar

Revised Clinical Institute Withdrawal Assessment for Alcohol. Source: Sullivan 1989 (*Br J Addict*).

## Items

Ten rater-scored items. Nine scored 0–7: nausea/vomiting, tremor, paroxysmal sweats, anxiety, agitation, tactile disturbances, auditory disturbances, visual disturbances, headache/fullness in head. One item scored 0–4: orientation and clouding of sensorium. Total range 0 to 67.

## Interpretation (commonly used)

| Score | Band |
|---|---|
| < 8 | Minimal |
| 8–15 | Mild to moderate |
| 16–20 | Moderate to severe |
| > 20 | Severe (increased risk of seizures and delirium tremens) |

The original instrument did not prescribe fixed thresholds; the score that triggers medication and the agent used are set by local protocol.

## Use restrictions

Requires a cooperative, communicative patient; unreliable in delirium, intubation, severe agitation, or communication barriers. A measure to guide symptom-triggered therapy, not a standalone protocol.
