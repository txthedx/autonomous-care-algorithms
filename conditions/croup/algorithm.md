# Westley Croup Score

Grades the severity of croup (laryngotracheobronchitis) in children. Source: Westley 1978 (*Am J Dis Child*).

## Items

| Item | Levels (points) |
|---|---|
| Level of consciousness | normal (0), disoriented (5) |
| Cyanosis | none (0), with agitation (4), at rest (5) |
| Stridor | none (0), with agitation (1), at rest (2) |
| Air entry | normal (0), decreased (1), markedly decreased (2) |
| Retractions | none (0), mild (1), moderate (2), severe (3) |

Total range: 0 to 17.

## Interpretation

| Score | Band |
|---|---|
| ≤ 2 | Mild |
| 3–5 | Moderate |
| 6–11 | Severe |
| ≥ 12 | Impending respiratory failure |

## Use restrictions

A grading tool, not a diagnosis; it does not exclude other causes of stridor (foreign body, epiglottitis). Severe and impending-failure scores warrant urgent intervention regardless of the exact number. Cyanosis and altered consciousness are heavily weighted late signs.
