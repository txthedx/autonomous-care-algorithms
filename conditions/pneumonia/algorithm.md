# Community-acquired pneumonia: CURB-65 and CRB-65

Two severity scores for adult CAP, derived and validated in Lim 2003 (Thorax) and endorsed by the British Thoracic Society 2009 guideline. Both are guides to disposition (outpatient versus inpatient versus higher-acuity), not substitutes for clinical judgment.

## CURB-65

Five criteria, one point each. Score range 0 to 5.

| Letter | Criterion | Threshold | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| C | Confusion | New disorientation in person, place, or time (or AMTS ≤ 8). | Lim 2003 |
| U | Urea | Serum urea > 7 mmol/L (BUN approximately > 19 mg/dL). | Lim 2003 |
| R | Respiratory rate | ≥ 30 breaths per minute. | Lim 2003 |
| B | Blood pressure | Systolic < 90 mmHg **or** diastolic ≤ 60 mmHg. | Lim 2003 |
| 65 | Age | ≥ 65 years. | Lim 2003 |

### Approximate 30-day mortality by score (Lim 2003 derivation)

| Score | Mortality (approximate) |
|---|---|
| 0 | ~0.7% |
| 1 | ~3.2% |
| 2 | ~13% |
| 3 | ~17% |
| 4 | ~41% |
| 5 | ~57% |

These are derivation-cohort estimates and vary across validation populations.

### Disposition bands (BTS 2009, NICE NG138)

| Score | Severity | Recommended disposition |
|---|---|---|
| 0 or 1 | Low | Likely suitable for outpatient treatment in patients without other concerning features. |
| 2 | Moderate | Consider hospital-supervised treatment; either short-stay inpatient or closely supervised outpatient if otherwise stable. |
| 3 | High | Hospital admission. |
| 4 or 5 | High, with ICU consideration | Hospital admission; assess for ICU or high-dependency care using IDSA/ATS 2007 criteria. |

## CRB-65

Identical to CURB-65 without the urea criterion. Score range 0 to 4. Intended for use in primary care and community settings where serum urea is not available.

### Approximate 30-day mortality by score (Lim 2003 derivation)

| Score | Mortality (approximate) |
|---|---|
| 0 | ~1.2% |
| 1 or 2 | ~8.5% |
| 3 or 4 | ~31% |

### Disposition bands

| Score | Severity | Recommended disposition |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Low | Likely suitable for outpatient treatment in patients without other concerning features. |
| 1 or 2 | Moderate | Consider hospital referral for assessment. |
| 3 or 4 | High | Urgent hospital admission. |

## Factors the scores do not capture

These should be assessed independently and may override the score-suggested disposition:

- **Oxygenation.** Hypoxia (commonly SpO₂ < 92% on room air in patients without chronic hypoxic disease) typically warrants hospital assessment regardless of score.
- **Radiographic findings.** Multilobar or bilateral disease raises risk.
- **Comorbidities.** CHF, COPD, CKD, liver disease, diabetes, immunocompromise.
- **Social factors.** Inability to take oral medications, isolation, substance use, homelessness.
- **Pregnancy.** Lower threshold for hospital assessment.
